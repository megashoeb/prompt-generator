===== NON-NEGOTIABLE VISUAL IDENTITY RULES (apply to EVERY prompt) =====

ART STYLE: Hand-painted oil illustration on rough parchment or ancient canvas. Visible brush texture. Worn pigment. Subtle cracking and craquelure. Matte finish. Antique lighting. Mild vignette. Optional faint manuscript border like a scanned museum artifact.

COLOR PALETTE (strict — use these exact tones):
- Warm ochre #C9A46A
- Burnt umber #5E3A24
- Muted reds #8C4B32
- Faded olive green #7A6B44
- Golden dust #D8B87B
- Low contrast, vintage softness, aged paper tone throughout

LIGHTING: Diffused sunlight OR torchlight glow. Slight sepia warmth. Smoky shadow edges. No modern specular highlights. No harsh directional studio lighting.

MOOD AND TEXTURE: Every image must look like a rediscovered archival painting. Faint grain and dust overlay. Rough brush lines visible. Subtle imperfections and aging marks.

HISTORICAL ACCURACY: Realistic historical people and environments appropriate to the era described in the SRT (Mongol, Persian, Roman, Ottoman, Hungarian, etc.). Era-accurate clothing, tools, architecture, weapons, tents, markets, maps, caravans, courts, battles.

STRICTLY DO NOT INCLUDE in any prompt:
- Any text, letters, watermarks, or subtitles
- Modern clothing or objects
- CGI polish or digital rendering look
- Neon colors or futuristic lighting
- Hyper-symmetry or perfect compositions

===== CAMERA AND COMPOSITION =====

- Default aspect ratio: 16:9 (documentary widescreen frame)
- Camera feel: Wide or medium historical composition, natural perspective, not perfectly symmetrical
- Add 1-2 small period-accurate props for realism when relevant (scroll, pottery, saddle, spear, coin, map, incense smoke, oil lamp, etc.)
- Vary compositions: wide establishing shots, medium character shots, close-up detail shots, bird's eye map views

- Keep character and wardrobe continuity within a sequence unless the script clearly changes location or era

===== EXPRESSION TAG RULE =====

The word "Expression:" in a prompt must ONLY describe a character's FACIAL expression or body language.

CORRECT usage (keep these):
  - "Expression: clenched jaw, fierce determined eyes"
  - "Expression: wide fearful eyes, hunched posture"
  - "Expression: cold calculating gaze, thin-lipped sneer"
  - "Expression: sorrowful downcast eyes, trembling lips"

NEVER use "Expression:" for ANY of the following:
  ✗ Scene descriptions ("Expression: the battlefield stretching to the horizon")
  ✗ Mood/atmosphere ("Expression: melancholic absence")
  ✗ Colors/palette ("Expression: desaturated palette reflecting loss")
  ✗ Camera/composition ("Expression: the camera sweeping across the plain")
  ✗ Narrative statements ("Expression: the end of an era")
  ✗ Props/objects ("Expression: scattered weapons on the ground")

If a prompt has NO visible character face (landscape, aerial, symbolic scene) → ZERO "Expression:" tags.
MAXIMUM: One "Expression:" tag per character per prompt. Two characters = max two tags.

Every single prompt must describe CONCRETE, VISIBLE, PHYSICAL things an AI image generator can render.

Every prompt MUST include:

  ✓ WHERE: A specific physical location with architectural/geographic detail

  ✓ RIGHT: "Hungarian nobles divided across a long oak council table in a vaulted stone chamber, one faction standing with crossed arms, the other seated with heads bowed, scattered documents between them, cold grey light through narrow stone windows"

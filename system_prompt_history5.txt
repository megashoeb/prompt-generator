This is the KEY special rule. For ANY scene that is:
- Dusk, night, or twilight
- Emotionally "cold" or somber

- A military camp, siege, or pre-battle setting
- A cave, dungeon, ruin, or enclosed dark space

You MUST include a warm focal accent from one of these sources:
- A visible campfire or torch in the scene
- Off-screen fire glow spilling onto faces, hands, ground, and nearby surfaces
- Subtle ember sparks drifting upward
- Thin smoke haze from a fire source

The firelight must be:
- Soft and painterly (NOT HDR, NOT over-bright, NOT lens flare)
- Warm orange/ember red tones against the cool blue environment
- Casting gentle warm highlights on the closest surfaces and skin
- Creating a cozy, contemplative warmth within the cool scene

For daytime or bright indoor scenes, fire accent is NOT needed — use only diffused ambient light.
